Tell a non-techincal friend about when you..

got blocked on a simple problem. What was the problem?

A simple digital logic circuit wasn't functioning as it should have. Turns out i had forgotten to supply power to one part of it.

What problem solving technique did you use?

Systematic backtracking of the problem. Isolating and testing sections.

How did you feel throughout the process?

Confused as to why the simple system was behaving erratically.
Frustrated as to why the individual parts were all working when tried outside of the systems.

What did you learn?

Double check the basics that can be checked quickly. It might be simple but it can still be easily mixed up.

Solved a problem in an elegant way. What was the problem?

Cant think of problem that I solved in a way I would say was very elegant.
Possible a coding test that involved itterating through numbers figuring out how many fit a certain property solved by calculating the answer from the requirements rather than checking numbers.

What problem solving technique did you use?

persistence and a bit of trial and error.

How did you feel throughout the process?

slightly disjointed like the simple solution should be right in front of me but just wasnt quite there.

What did you learn?

Approaching a problem from a completely different angle can yeild intersting results.

How confident do you feel using the following problem solving techniques and procesess.

Pseudocode- Confident with this as it is useful for seperating things out into seperate parts.

Trying Something- Used more as a last resort as I prefer having more knowledge of what im doing and why.

Rubber ducky method- Used confidently with pseudocode to double check you know what you are doing.

Reading error messages- Relies a fair bit on how well the error messages are setup. some languages are better than others.

Console logging- Good for aligning what you think should be happening with what is actually happening.

Googling- Best used when you have a grasp of what you want to get done.

Asking your peers for help- Very helpful for situations where you want a fresh pair of eyes to check you didnt mix up something basic.

Asking coaches for help- Useful for when you are close but arnt quite understanding something.

Improving your process with reflection- Very useful but often overlooked as I often have a habit of quickly throwing myself at the next problem.
